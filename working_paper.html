Working Paper

Portfolio Optimization using Anomalies: A Deep Learning Approach

Aman Foujdar1, Sandeep Juneja1, Atish Kumar2, Nagpurnanand Prabhala3, Sidharth Wagle1

1Safexpress Centre for Data Learning and Decision Sciences, Ashoka University, India

2Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi, India

3Carey Business School, Johns Hopkins University, USA

Latest Version: December 2025 •

Abstract

The explanatory power of Fama-French factors in capturing stock returns variation is well established in the literature. Furthermore, recent advances in deep learning techniques for time-series forecasting have outperformed regression-based models on a variety of datasets.

In this paper, we construct factors related to Indian equity markets leveraging annual firm balance-sheet and daily stock level data. In addition, we propose a novel approach to portfolio optimization by training a deep learning model using Fama-French factors. We select the top 300 firms on the basis of market capitalization and build a long-short trading strategy where the portfolio is rebalanced every month. Our trading strategy generates an economically significant alpha and is easily implementable in practice.

Key Findings

01

iTransformer Generates Alpha

The proposed iTransformer trading strategy generates a significant alpha, achieving an average monthly return of 1.25% and a Sharpe ratio of 2.1.

02

Small-Winner Portfolio Outperforms

Stocks classified as "small" in size and "winners" in momentum delivered a 38% CAGR over 11 years, substantially outperforming the benchmark NIFTY50 index.

03

Equal-Weighting Boosts Returns

Equal-weighting the portfolio increases returns by reducing concentration risk and improving diversification, leading to a more robust and stable investment strategy.

Research Overview

Motivation

While the explanatory power of Fama-French factors is well-established, traditional linear models often fail to capture the complex, non-linear relationships in stock returns, an area where deep learning techniques are increasingly proving superior.

Furthermore, existing global studies often overlook India specific nuances, such as the high concentration of small firms in India, making broad methodologies difficult to replicate locally. This paper addresses these gaps by constructing an Indian-specific dataset and applying a novel deep learning architecture (iTransformer) to optimize portfolios, aiming to bridge the divide between theoretical asset pricing and practical, investment-friendly trading strategies.

Data & Methodology

We give a brief overview of our data pipeline:

  • Data Universe & Source: The study analyzes the top 300 firms listed on the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) by market capitalization, covering approximately 90% of the total market cap to ensure liquidity. The data is sourced from the CMIE Prowess DX database, spanning over 25 years from April 2000 to March 2025
  • Factor Construction: The authors construct Fama-French factors like Value, Size, Momentum, Investment, and Operating Profitability, tailored to the Indian context (e.g., using an 80th percentile cutoff for "Big" vs. "Small" firms rather than the median).
  • Model Architecture: The strategy utilizes the iTransformer, a time-series transformer model that employs an "inverted embedding" to capture multivariate correlations between assets and features rather than just temporal dependencies.
  • Trading Strategy: The model is trained to minimize the negative Sharpe ratio, outputting a net-zero, long-short portfolio that is rebalanced monthly.

Main Results & Implications

The deep learning strategy proved highly effective, generating an average monthly return of 1.25% and a Sharpe ratio of 2.1. In the test period (2023–2025), the model achieved an annualized return of 16% with a maximum monthly loss of only 1.66%.

A key finding was the dominance of the "Small-Winner" portfolio (small-cap stocks with high momentum), which delivered a 38% CAGR over 11 years, vastly outperforming the NIFTY50 benchmark. The study implies that transformer-based architectures are viable for empirical asset pricing in emerging markets and that equal-weighted portfolios are superior to value-weighted ones in India for capturing the size premium.
